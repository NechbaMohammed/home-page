Welcome!

I'm an enthusiastic undergraduate student at ENSIAS, Mohammed V University in Morocco, specializing in Artificial Intelligence. My fervor for AI research and innovation propels me forward.

I am currently engaged in two research projects:

  • Computation of Vapnik Chervonenkis dimension for non-discrete settings.
  • Boosting in Robust Adversarial Learning within Agnostic settings.


I've also designed a GPU-accelerated Gradient Descent Logistic Regression algorithm, accepted at The IEEE International Conference on Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, and Internet of Things (AIBThings).

Prior to ENSIAS, I have obtained my bachelor degree in Applied Mathematics at Ibno Tofail University, ranking first. My thesis defense centered on Hausdorff measure, diving into its core in fractal geometry, defining its outer and proper forms, and delving into properties like monotonicity. It also includes an application to the Cantor ternary set, illustrating how Hausdorff measure quantifies geometric intricacies in fractals.

My journey underlines my commitment to AI advancement, blending theoretical and practical skills to lead pioneering research.
